# Code of Conduct — NullByte Academy Advanced Defensive Systems Engineering Program

**Document Classification:** Community Standards
**Version:** 1.0
**Maintained By:** NullByte Academy Maintainer Council
**Effective Date:** 2026-02-01

---

## Purpose

NullByte Academy is a professional educational and research program. The standards in this document apply to all participants: students, contributors, instructors, reviewers, and institutional partners. They govern conduct in all program spaces: the repository, GitHub Issues, Pull Requests, Discussions, lab environments, and any communications conducted under the NullByte Academy name.

These standards exist for one reason: to ensure that the program's technical and ethical mission — advancing defensive security knowledge — is not undermined by conduct that degrades the quality of collaboration, the safety of participants, or the integrity of research.

---

## 1. Expected Conduct

Participants in this program are expected to:

**Engage with technical substance.** Technical disagreements must be grounded in evidence: citations, verified test results, or reasoned argument from first principles. Asserting that something is wrong without explaining why is not a technical position.

**Communicate with precision and honesty.** Do not overstate your expertise or the confidence of your claims. If you are uncertain, say so. If your analysis may be incomplete, acknowledge it. In security research, false confidence causes harm.

**Attribute others' work.** Every idea, code sample, research finding, or analysis derived from an external source must be attributed. This is both an ethical obligation and a practical standard: it allows reviewers to verify your claims against the original source.

**Operate within the ethical framework.** All work produced through or associated with this program must comply with the standards in `03_ETHICS.md`. This includes lab exercises, research documents, contributions to the curriculum, and any external work that cites the program.

**Accept and give feedback constructively.** Code reviews, editorial comments, and assessment feedback are technical communications. They are directed at content, not at people. Receiving critical feedback without defensiveness and providing it without condescension are both professional skills this program expects.

---

## 2. Prohibited Conduct

The following are prohibited in all program spaces and in work associated with this program:

**Technical misconduct:**

- Submitting content that is factually incorrect and has not been reviewed before submission, without acknowledging the review gap
- Representing others' analysis, code, or research as your own
- Circumventing the peer review or assessment process
- Using program resources or credentials to conduct unauthorized security testing outside the program's permissioned lab environments

**Conduct toward people:**

- Harassment, intimidation, or sustained unwelcome contact of any kind
- Discriminatory remarks, slurs, or behavior based on any personal characteristic
- Personal attacks in the context of technical disagreement
- Deliberate exclusion of participants from collaboration based on personal rather than technical grounds
- Publishing private information about any participant without their consent

**Ethical violations:**

- Using curriculum knowledge to conduct unauthorized access to computer systems
- Constructing or distributing weaponized attack tooling derived from curriculum concepts
- Misrepresenting the program's endorsement or association in external communications
- Pressuring any participant to violate the ethical standards of the program

## 3. Enforcement

### Reporting

Conduct violations may be reported to the Maintainer Council at `mrwhite4939@gmail.com`. Reports must include:

- A description of the specific conduct and when it occurred
- The program space in which it occurred (GitHub, email, lab environment, etc.)
- Any supporting evidence (links, screenshots, logs)
- The reporter's preferred contact information for follow-up

Reports are treated as confidential. The identity of the reporter is not disclosed to the subject of the report without the reporter's consent.

### Response Process

Upon receiving a report, the Maintainer Council will:

1. Acknowledge receipt within 48 hours
2. Conduct an initial assessment within 7 days
3. Take one of the following actions, documented in the council's decision record:
   - No action (report not substantiated)
   - Warning issued to the subject
   - Temporary restriction from program spaces
   - Permanent removal from the program and all associated spaces

Decisions are final. The council does not enter into extended debate with subjects about enforcement decisions. Subjects who believe a decision was made in error may submit a written appeal within 14 days, which will be reviewed by a council member who was not involved in the original decision.

### Responsibility of Maintainers

Maintainers are held to the same conduct standards as all other participants. Reports of maintainer misconduct are referred to the senior maintainer or, in the absence of one, to an external designated reviewer identified in MAINTAINERS.md.

---

## 4. Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all program-affiliated contexts. It applies to:

- The GitHub repository and all its associated spaces (Issues, PRs, Discussions, Actions)
- All communications conducted using program contact addresses or under the program's name
- Lab environments provided or referenced by the program
- Any event, workshop, or presentation conducted under the NullByte Academy name

It does not apply to participants' conduct in entirely unaffiliated personal contexts, unless that conduct directly affects the safety, reputation, or integrity of the program or its participants.

## 5. Relationship to Other Documents

This Code of Conduct governs personal conduct. The ethical standards governing research and lab work are defined in `03_ETHICS.md`. The contribution process standards are defined in `CONTRIBUTING.md`. The governance process for program decisions is defined in `GOVERNANCE.md`.

In cases of apparent conflict between this document and `03_ETHICS.md`, both sets of standards apply and the more specific provision governs the specific situation.
